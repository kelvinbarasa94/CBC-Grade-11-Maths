      🔗
    • Determine terms and common difference in an arithmetic sequence and series
      🔗
    🔗
    Grade Level
    🔗
    11
    🔗

    🔗

    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    Learner Experience 6.2.1.

    In Groups, study and explore the following sequence
    🔗
    \(3,7,11,15 ...\)
    🔗
    Find the next three terms of the sequence? Share with other groups your findings.
    🔗
    The next term is obtained by adding \(4\) to the preceeding term. \(4\) is called the common difference (\(d\)) of the sequence. This is the difference betweeen any two consecutive terms of the sequence.
    🔗
    🔗

    Key Takeaway 6.2.1.

    Any sequence of numbers which have a common difference is called an arithmetic sequence. In an arithmetic sequence, the first term is \(a\) and the common difference is \(d\text{.}\)
    🔗
    Can you explore how to find the second, third, forth, ... terms given the first term?
    🔗
    Notice that: The general form of an arithmetic sequence is \(a, a+d, a+2d, a+3d ...\text{.}\)
    🔗
    In the general form,
    🔗
    \begin{align*} 1^{st} term \amp =\; a \\ 2^{nd} term \amp =\; a+d \\ 3^{rd} term \amp =\; a+2d \\ 4^{th} term \amp =\; a+3d \end{align*}
    🔗
    What is the \(10^{th}\) term?
    🔗
    How do we find the \(n^{th}\) term of an arithmetic sequence?
    🔗
    🔗

    Example 6.2.3.

    Given the arithmetic sequence \(4,11,18, ... find:\)
    🔗
    1. The common difference.
      🔗
      🔗
    2. The \(6^{th}\) term.
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗

    Solution.

    1. The common difference
      🔗
      \begin{align*} d \amp =\; 11-4 = 18-11 \\ \amp =\; 7 \end{align*}
      🔗
      🔗
    2. The \(n^{th} \;\text{term}\; = a + (n-1)d \)
      🔗
      \begin{align*} 6^{th} \;\text{term}\; \amp =\; 4+(6-1)7 \\ \amp =\; 39 \end{align*}
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    Example 6.2.4.

    The \(20^{th}\) term of an arithmetic sequence is \(60\) and \(16^{th}\) term is \(20\text{.}\) Find the first term and the common difference.
    🔗

    Solution.

    \begin{align*} \text{The} \; 20^{th}\;\text{term}\; \amp =\; a+(20-1)d =\amp\; 60 \\ \therefore a+19d \amp =\; 60 ......(i) \\ \text{The} \; 16^{th}\;\text{term}\; \amp =\; a+(16-1)d =\amp\; 20 \\ \therefore a+15d \amp =\; 20 ......(ii) \end{align*}
    🔗
    \((i) - (ii) \) gives;
    🔗
    \begin{align*} 4d \amp =\; 40 \\ \amp =\; 10 \end{align*}
    🔗
    \begin{align*} \text{But}\; a+15d \amp =\; 20 \\ \therefore a+15\times10 \amp =\; 20 \\ a + 150 \amp =\; 20 \\ a =\amp\; -130 \end{align*}
    🔗
    Hence, the first term is \(-130\) and the common difference is \(10\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    Example 6.2.5.

    Find the number of terms in the sequence \(-3,0,3,...,54\)
    🔗

    Solution.

    The \(n^{th}\) term is \(a+(n-1)d \)
    🔗
    \begin{align*} a \amp =\; -3, d = 3 \\ n^{th} \;\text{term}\; \amp =\; 54 \\ \therefore -3+(n-1)3 \amp =\; 54 \\ 3(n-1)\amp =\; 57 \\ n-1 \amp =\; 19 \\ n \amp =\; 20 \end{align*}
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗
